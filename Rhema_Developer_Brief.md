## What You Are Building

Rhema is a Christian daily declaration app. The premise is simple and theologically precise: God has already spoken about every situation a believer will ever face. The Bible contains his Word — complete, personal, already given. The user's role is to declare it and believe it.

This is not a devotional app. It is not a Bible reading plan. It is not a prayer guide written by someone else. It is a personal repository of God's Word, rendered in first person, spoken by the user in their own voice, meeting them exactly where they are each morning.

Every declaration in the app is a Bible verse transformed into first person with minimum editorial intervention. "For I know the plans I have for you" becomes "For you know the plans you have for me." The Word is unchanged. The person speaking it is the user. The source reference is always shown — this is what separates Rhema from an affirmation app. Every declaration is traceable to scripture.

---

## The Core Experience — Three Screens

The entire morning experience is three screens. It takes fifteen seconds minimum. It should feel like the most important fifteen seconds of the day.

### Screen 1 — The Foundation Statement

A full-screen opening statement. Not interactive. Just truth. Displayed each time the app opens:

> *He has already given His Word about you.*  
> *For every season. Every situation. Every day.*  
> *You just need to declare it and believe.*

The user taps anywhere to proceed.

### Screen 2 — The Morning Question

A single question. Three options. One tap.

**How is today?**

Three equal circles or cards:

- **Burdened** — I am carrying something heavy
- **Hopeful** — I am trusting for something not yet seen
- **Joyful** — I am full because of who He is

These are not a mood spectrum from negative to positive. They are three simultaneous spiritual postures — a person can be burdened and joyful at the same time. The selection matches the user to a declaration suited to their posture. There is no wrong answer.

### Screen 3 — The Declaration

The declaration fills the screen. Clean. Uncluttered. Nothing competes for attention.

The declaration text is large, bold, and navy. The source reference sits beneath it in smaller, styled text. Below the reference, the prayer appears — either as a preview line (free tier logic, for future implementation) or in full.

The user reads it. Declares it. The morning is complete.

## What the App Must Feel Like

This is the single most important brief requirement. The app must feel like a private moment, not a product.

The user is not browsing content. They are not completing a task. They are not being coached or taught. They are having a personal encounter with God's Word — first thing in the morning, in the quiet, before the day lands on them.

Every design decision should serve this feeling. Stillness over motion. Space over density. Weight over lightness. The declaration should feel like something worth stopping for.

**Specific qualities to aim for:**

- **Navy and warm cream/gold palette** — not clinical white, not dark mode black. Warm, grounded, intimate.
- **Serif typography for declarations** — Georgia or similar. The Word deserves gravitas.
- **Sans-serif for UI elements** — clean contrast against the declaration text.
- **Generous white space** — the declaration breathes. Nothing crowds it.
- **No gamification** — no streaks, no badges, no progress bars. None.
- **No social elements** — this is a private moment. There is no feed, no community wall.
- **No notifications or urgency** — the app does not pressure. It invites.
- **Transitions are gentle** — slow fades, not snappy slides. Reverence in motion.

The model for the felt quality is a beautifully designed personal journal — not a productivity tool, not a social app, not a news feed.

## Feature Scope for the Prototype

Build exactly what is listed here. Nothing more. The prototype exists to validate the core experience — three screens, declaration surfaced by bucket, prayer visible. Additional features will be layered in as the product matures.

### Must Have

- Screen 1: Foundation statement — full screen, tap to proceed
- Screen 2: Bucket selection — Burdened / Hopeful / Joyful, one tap
- Screen 3: Declaration matched to selected bucket, source reference shown, full prayer shown
- Navigation: user can go back to change their bucket selection
- On returning to Screen 2 a second time in the same session, the app remembers the previous selection but allows changing it

### Must Not Have (in this prototype)

- Login or account creation
- Any data persistence beyond the current session
- Push notifications
- Sharing functionality
- Settings screen
- Onboarding flow
- Audio playback
- Journal or note-taking
- Browsing or search
- Any paywall or pricing UI

## Screen Layout Guidance

### Screen 1 — Foundation Statement

- Full viewport height
- Vertically and horizontally centred text
- Three lines, each on its own line
- First and third lines bold — *He has already given His Word about you* and *You just need to declare it and believe*
- Middle line lighter weight — *For every season. Every situation. Every day.*
- Small subtle tap prompt at the bottom: "Tap to continue" or a chevron — barely visible, not urgent

### Screen 2 — Bucket Selection

- Question centred near the top third: **How is today?**
- Three options below — equal weight, equal size, stacked vertically on mobile or in a row on wider screens
- Each option: the word (Burdened / Hopeful / Joyful) and optionally a one-line description beneath it
- Selected state: clear but not garish — a border, a fill, a check. The user should know their selection was received.
- A gentle "confirm" or the selection itself navigates forward — either works

### Screen 3 — Declaration

- Source reference small, styled, above or below the declaration — below preferred
- Declaration text: large, bold, centred or left-aligned — left-aligned preferred for longer declarations
- Clear visual separator between declaration and prayer sections
- Prayer section: label "PRAYER" small and uppercase above the prayer text
- Prayer text: italic, slightly smaller than declaration, same colour family
- Back navigation available — subtle, top left

## What This Prototype Will Prove

Three things need to be true for the prototype to succeed:

**1. The declaration lands.** When a user sees their declaration on Screen 3, it should feel personal and immediate. Not generic. Not like content. Like God spoke directly to them this morning.

**2. The prayer completes the moment.** After the declaration, the prayer should feel like a natural response — the user speaking back to God in the light of what was declared. Not a separate feature. A continuation.

**3. The bucket selection feels honest.** Burdened / Hopeful / Joyful should feel like a genuine question, not a mood filter. The user should tap one without hesitation and feel accurately represented.

If all three land, the core product works. Everything else — journal, search, anniversary returns, audio, paid tier — is built on top of this foundation.

## What Comes After the Prototype

When the prototype is validated, the build sequence continues:

1. Expand declaration database from 10 to 365+ (extraction is in progress — 1,100+ declarations already built across 11 books of the Bible)
2. Add on-device history — 7-day cache, no login required
3. Add journal feature — paid tier, "What is this saying to you today?"
4. Add situation search — paid tier, "I am facing..."
5. Convert single-file React to PWA with offline support
6. Package for Android via Capacitor, then iOS

The single-file React prototype is not throwaway work. The component structure, data model, and design language established here carry forward into every subsequent phase. Build it with that in mind.
